Why This Analysis Matters

Understanding migration patterns is crucial for humanitarian planning, policy development, and addressing root causes of displacement across Africa.

Research Objective

To analyze 76 years of African migration and refugee movements, identifying patterns, drivers, and humanitarian hotspots to inform evidence-based decision making for aid organizations, policymakers, and researchers.

Scope & Impact

Comprehensive analysis covering all 54 African countries, tracking over 150 major conflicts and displacement events that affected millions of people across different time periods and regions.

Key Insights

Identified geographic hotspots, temporal peaks during decolonization and post-Cold War periods, demographic impacts on vulnerable populations, and evolving drivers from political to climate-related displacement.

Humanitarian Value

Provides actionable insights for humanitarian organizations to anticipate needs, allocate resources effectively, and develop targeted interventions for displaced populations across Africa.
